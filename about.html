About Me

Born and raised in Indiana, technology has always fascinated me. I was always interested in technology, starting with family and typical hobbies like video games, which led me to computers and coding in high school.

In college, I developed my love for tech and expanded my understanding of software and coding through Computer Science. Ultimately, I would end up in Informatics, transforming what I knew into real-world scenarios that needed solving through the means of technology. This drove my passion further and helped me stay on a path of success, proven by past grades and projects.

Why Informatics?

Informatics, to me at least, is the bridge between people and technology. Not everyone understands technology, and for many of us, learning something new isn’t always so simple. Furthermore, teaching someone a new technology can be difficult in certain scenarios. Without that bridge to better connect people with that technology, users get stuck in the dreaded "black box" scenario.

This goes beyond the user perspective, however. Businesses that understand their consumers and provide the right deliverables will be successful, but businesses are also run by people with different skills and mindsets. Businessmen may not be on the same wavelength as coders, but things like IT and Informatics are the middle ground of that wavelength.

What makes me love Informatics is that it’s somewhat of a wild card. A wild card (in Uno, for example) can completely change the way you play. It can be applied to so many different topics, from sports to medicine, art, nature, media, etc. I found myself, and others within Informatics, excelling at many of these topics because Informatics is about the impact of technology and how we make something truly helpful out of it.

Other Thoughts

The past years within school have taught me a lot about myself and challenged me to push myself to do what I thought wasn’t possible.

To this day, I’m still pushing to meet new challenges that develop my work ethic, thought process and even my personality.

Even with all I have accomplished, there’s so much more I can do, and I hope to continue putting myself out there and see what I’m truly capable of.
